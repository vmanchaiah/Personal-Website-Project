Awards

Congressional Award

Link to Congressional Award Page

Information About Award

The Congressional Award had various levels that you can work towards. There are four program areas in which you must work towards: voluntary service, personal development, physical fitness, and expedition/ exploration. You must meet the program-requirements for each of the four different areas to earn the award.

Work Done for Award

For this award I got volunteer hours from volunteering at various places in my community such as the school and the local food pantry. For personal development I worked to improve my violin skills by using target practice. For physical fitness I trained for and successfully ran a 10k. For my expedition I planned a trip to Oklahoma City where I explored the city and all it has to offer.

Work Done for Award

For this award I volunteered over a hundred hours in my community at events such as Christmas in Springboro, Oktoberfest, Boro Fest, and many more. I also received a large amount of volunteer hours working towards my gold award project. I was validated by the Girl Scouts organization.

Girl Scouts Gold Award

Link to Girl Scouts Gold Award Page

Information About Award

The Girl Scouts Gold Award is awarded to those who tackle an issue in their community. The project needs to sustainable even after the project is completed by the Girl Scout. She must show leadership and work with the members of the community.

Work Done for Award

For this award I set up a lending library at my local food pantry. I collected books from people in the community and got old books from local libraries. I wanted to find an easy way for the people who did not have enough time to visit the library have access to books.
